What is Sudoku

Sudoku is a logic-based number placement puzzle.

It is not just a game, but a fun mental workout!

A Brief History

  • Game basis originated in the 18 century, from mathematician Leonard Euler.
  • Called “Latin Squares”, it was a square grid filled with symbols that occur only once in each row and column.
  • One of the many variations of “Latin Squares” was then perfected by Maki Kaji to become the game we know today.
  • The name comes from the Japanese phrase “Suuji wa dokushin ni kagiru” meaning “numbers should be single”.
  • Sudoku puzzles were first mass printed in 1984.
  • In the early 2000s, Sudoku began its international journey, becoming the ppular puzzle it is now.

How to Play Sudoku

Classic Rules

  1. Sudoku is played in a 9x9 grid. Meaning there are 9 rows and 9 columns.
  2. Additionally, the grid is also divided in 3x3 cell groups called boxes.
  3. The aim of the game is to fill the grid with the numbers 1-9.
  4. Numbers must appear only once in each row, column and 3x3 box.
  5. Do not guess!

Variations

Classic Sudoku Rules Still Apply

Diagonals

Cells along the longest diagonals, going from row 1 column 1 to row 9 colum 9 and row 9 column 1 to row 1 column 9, do not repeat

Killer

The grid contains cages, regions of orthogonally connected cells marked with a dashed line.

Cages can have different shapes and size.

Numbers inside a cage do not repeat and sum to the number in its top left corner.

Kropki Dots

Pairs of numbers next to a white dot must be consecutive, i.e.: 5-6.

Pairs of numbers next to a black dots have a 1:2 ratio, i.e.: 4-8.

Fog (of War)

The grid is covered in fog and only some of the cells are visible.

Filling in correct digits will clear the fog in the adiacent cells, revealing more numbers to help solve the grid.

Fog sudoku often make use of other variations, like killer or kropki, to create more challenging puzzles.

This type of sudoku is only available to play digitally, as it requires a program to remove the fog from the grid.
